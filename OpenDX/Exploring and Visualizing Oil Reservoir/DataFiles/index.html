Our Experiment Details

Effective oil and gas production often requires (among other things), optimization of well placement and efficient exploration of possible production strategies. Challenges however are abounds, which includes geologic uncertainty, operational flexibility, and large &amp; detail flow models. Therefore, engineers often resolve to a cheaper, less risky and relatively fast ways of collecting relevant data for study and analysis. Important data such as the measurements of pressure and saturation of crucial entities e.g. water and oil, may help oil companies optimize well placement.
This exercise is to investigate the behaviour of the water and oil properties in the simulated domain.   The questions we try to answer are as follows :

    1. How does the three measurements vary in the simulated domain?

    2. What is the pattern of the oil’s pressure over a period of say 10, 25 and 50 years ? Similar question applies to water data?

    3. Are there any relation between water’s parameters and the oil’s parameter ?
